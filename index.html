This website is a tribute to Budots, a grassroots electronic dance genre that originated from Davao City, Philippines. DJ Love (also known as Sherwin Tuna) was one of the genre’s pioneers, who started creating Budots music from his internet cafe in the mid 2000s.

The background uses YouTube Trends data to visualize interest in Budots over time. The outer circle represents Budots' popularity in the Philippines since 2009, while the inner circle shows interest in the United States.

Budots is rooted in the creativity of low-income Filipino communities, produced on pirated FL Studio software and blasted at street parties across the Philippines. Despite its cultural significance, Budots has often been dismissed due to classist assumptions that devalue accessible, grassroots art. These attitudes reflect a broader cultural bias that glorifies Western artistic standards while overlooking local expressions of joy, resilience, and innovation. Yet Budots has proven its mainstream appeal, both in the Philippines and worldwide. Deeply infused with a DIY spirit, Budots challenges conventional Western preconceptions of art and is a testament to Filipino ingenuity.
